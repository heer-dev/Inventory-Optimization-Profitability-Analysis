# 📊 Insights & Findings

## 🔴 Profitability Analysis

* The business generated **$177K in total sales** and **$22.5K in profit**, resulting in an overall profit margin of **12.7%**
* Several products are generating **significant losses**, with some exceeding **-$3000 per product** 
* Many low-margin products show **extremely negative profit margins (below -150%)**, indicating pricing or discounting issues
* Losses are not isolated—they appear across multiple categories, especially in furniture and office supplies

---

## 🟡 Inventory Performance

* Several products have extremely low sales volume (**only 1 unit sold**), indicating **dead stock risk**
* At the same time, a small number of products consistently drive higher sales (top products selling 15–22 units)
* Inventory distribution is highly uneven:

  * Few products = high demand
  * Majority = low or no movement

👉 This indicates poor inventory planning

---

## 📦 Product Performance

* Top-performing products (e.g., Canon Copier, Ativa Shredder) generate **high profit and revenue consistently** 
* However, some **high-revenue products are still loss-making**, showing poor pricing strategy
* A clear imbalance exists:

  * High-value products → profitable
  * Bulk low-value products → low contribution

---

## 📍 Regional Performance

* **West region** is the strongest performer:

  * Highest sales and profit
  * Profit margin ~18%

* **Central region is a major concern**:

  * Negative profit (~ -$3,500)
  * Profit margin ~ -25%

👉 This indicates operational or pricing inefficiencies in that region

---

## 🧾 Category & Sub-Category Insights

* **Technology category** is the most profitable (~19% margin)
* **Furniture category has very low profitability (~3%)**
* Some sub-categories are highly problematic:

  * Tables → negative profit
  * Binders → heavily negative margin (~ -19%)

👉 Not all products within a category perform equally

---

## 💸 Discount Impact Analysis

* Discounting has a **direct negative impact on profitability**
* At **0% discount → ~31% profit margin**
* At **30%+ discount → profits become negative**
* Extreme discounts (70–80%) result in:

  * Massive losses
  * No significant sales improvement

👉 Discounts are destroying profitability

---

## 📈 Sales Trend & Seasonality

* Sales show **high volatility month-to-month**
* Some months generate strong revenue but **negative profit**

  * Example: July 2014 → high sales but heavy loss
* Strong growth observed in:

  * 2017 (especially March & September spikes)

👉 Growth exists, but profitability is inconsistent

---

## 📊 Growth & Performance Trends

* Month-over-month growth fluctuates heavily:

  * Sudden spikes (>1000%) followed by sharp declines
* Business lacks stable growth pattern
* Running total shows consistent long-term growth:

  * Reached ~$177K total sales by end of 2017

👉 Business is growing, but not efficiently

---

## 🧠 Key Business Problems Identified

1. Loss-making products draining profit
2. Excessive discounting reducing margins
3. Poor inventory management (dead stock + uneven demand)
4. Underperforming region (Central)
5. Unstable revenue and profit trends

---

## 💡 Recommendations

* Discontinue or reprice loss-making products
* Limit discounts above 30%
* Optimize inventory by reducing dead stock
* Increase focus on high-performing products
* Investigate and improve Central region strategy
* Implement data-driven pricing and inventory planning
